# Phase 8 Novelty and Clarity Revision Plan

Date: 2026-06-11

Objective: raise reviewer-perceived novelty and clarity above the v7 plateau by changing the manuscript's structure, not by adding new empirical claims.

## Targeted Diagnosis

- Novelty was obscured by defensive framing and by repeated statements of what the paper does not claim.
- Clarity was weakened by ledger-style PC/C identifiers in the main narrative.
- Closest-prior positioning was present in prose but lacked a compact comparison marker.

## Executed Changes

- Front-loaded three positive contribution names in both manuscript drafts:
  - measurement-floor admissibility gate
  - typed domain-inadmissibility verdict
  - MR-class-to-fault-class diagnostic
- Added a closest-prior positioning marker in the IST related-work section, contrasting Reichert et al., Eniser et al., Duque-Torres / MetaTrimmer, and what this paper adds.
- Converted the claim-to-evidence map from internal PC/C ledger labels to reviewer-facing claim names while preserving `PC10-seeded-fault-detection` as the seeded-fault evidence anchor.
- Moved the runtime PC/C mapping out of the main narrative and pointed readers to `claim-ledger.yml`.
- Rewrote repeated defensive disclaimers into scope, boundary, or future-work statements.

## Guardrails

- No empirical numbers were changed.
- No new claims were added beyond the already-validated FNO, PINN, MGN, operator-floor, and fault-catalog evidence.
- The canonical blocked-list language remains in place.
- The revision targets readability and positioning only; evidence validation remains unchanged.
